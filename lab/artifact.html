OVMG Licensing Lab

Companion to Grammaticality de-idealized.

A speech community never votes on what’s grammatical. Evidence just accumulates: each occasion where an expression could be used ends with someone using it, using a competitor instead, or talking around it. Pick a case and watch what a learner could conclude from that evidence, and how firmly.

What’s happening

step 0

Nudge the learner

Presents the expression in a supportive context. If the verdict rests on thin evidence, the guess jumps; if hundreds of observations back it, nothing moves. That’s why repetition and framing budge some judgments and not others.

What the learner concludes

Solid line: the best guess at how much of the community licenses the expression. Shaded band: what the evidence so far still allows. Dotted: the path if evidence arrived exactly on average.

The status map (paper fig. 2)

High and right: licensed. High and left: confidently out. Low anywhere: unsettled, because nothing happens (starved) or because what happens is uninformative (avoidance). A preempted gap and a winnerless cell can share the same low guess; they part on the vertical axis, and that difference is what predicts framing effects, satiation, and change.

For readers of the paper: best guess = posterior mean C; weight of evidence = concentration ν; band = 95% credible interval; the sliders set N, π, ρ⋆, ω, δm, feeding the streams s = N·π and p = N·(1−π)·(1−ω)·ρ⋆ of eqs. 44–47. The preempted-gap and starved presets reproduce the paper’s Figure 4 exactly in expectation; a sampled run varies around it (Resample draws another community). Preset values are illustrative modelling choices; the qualitative profiles follow the paper’s worked examples.
